# Story Rules

## Age calibration

### 12–24 months
- 8–10 pages
- 1 short line per page
- 200–300 words max across the full story
- sensory language, repetition, animal sounds, familiar people
- no conflict, villains, jeopardy, or intense surprises
- end with calm and sleep

### 2–4 years
- 10–12 pages
- simple problem → gentle solution arc
- repetitive phrases the child can echo
- mild humor and friendly helpers
- end with calm and sleep

### 5–7 years
- 12–15 pages
- clearer narrative arc
- mild suspense only
- child solves the problem actively
- end safely and warmly

### 8+
- 15–20 pages
- more plot and twists
- still bedtime-calibrated, not overstimulating

## Writing rules
- Use the child’s real name throughout.
- Keep the child active, never passive.
- Prefer sensory detail over abstract description.
- Include short dialogue when it helps.
- Keep each page to one beat of the story.
- Keep modern brands, screens, and internet culture out of the story world unless the parent explicitly asks.
- Always land the ending in sleep, calm, cuddles, or winding down.

## Safety rules
- Avoid fear, monsters, death, injury, separation panic, punishment, and loud conflict unless the parent explicitly asks for older-child material.
- Respect the parent’s “avoid” list.
- If unsure, make the story gentler, simpler, and shorter.

## Character profile fields
Store and reuse:
- child name
- age
- appearance: hair, skin tone, eyes, features, default outfit
- favorite things
- recurring family members with brief descriptions
- fears / themes to avoid

## Output structure
- title page
- 8–15 illustrated story pages depending on age
- one line of story text per page for toddler mode
- optional narration file if requested
